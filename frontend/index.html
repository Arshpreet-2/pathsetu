Why this tab exists
Your biggest supplier risk isn't the suppliers you see. It's the ones you don't.
Existing platforms monitor your Tier 1 suppliers — the big vendors who sign your contracts. But those vendors depend on a long tail of small Tier 2/3 suppliers you never see. When something breaks upstream — a cold-chain failure in Nashik, a shock event in Firozabad, a factory offline in Bhagalpur — it surfaces as your problem weeks later, when an order fails.

The gap is not technology cost. It's reporting infrastructure. Small suppliers can't afford IoT. They do have smartphones. PathSetu turns those phones into the sensor layer — location, shock, photo, voice, even factory machine sound — and lifts the hidden 70% into view. For specialized cargo (cold-chain pharma, fragile electronics, high-value last-mile), optional hardware add-ons extend the phone with BLE tags or GPS trackers (see Impact & ROI tab for the three-tier economics).
Sources: Over 90% of India's cold chain is fragmented and privately owned (Maersk / Indian Pharmaceutical Alliance, 2024). 20% of India's temperature-sensitive healthcare products arrive damaged due to broken cold chains (Intelsius / manufacturingchemist.com, 2024). Logistics cost 7.9% of GDP, targeting reduction via Tier 2/3 visibility (FICCI-Grant Thornton, 2026).

Use case spotlight
Vaccine cold chain — where Tier 2/3 visibility becomes a public health outcome
Vaccines in India often travel through Tier 2/3 cold-chain operators — small transporters covering the last 50 km from district depot to primary health centre. When the reefer truck fails somewhere in that last mile, no one knows until the vaccine arrives at its destination, often hours later. By then, doses are already compromised.

With a phone-based BLE temperature tag on each vaccine box, the moment temperature crosses the safe range (2–8°C for most vaccines), both the district depot and the destination PHC are alerted in real time. Doses can be recalled or diverted before administration. One workflow. Three SDGs.
Workflow
Depot dispatch → Phone pairs with BLE tag → Transit upload every 5 min → Temp breach = instant alert → PHC quarantines before use

What you see here
Who pays for Tier 2/3 visibility? The anchor buyer. Here's why the math works.
This calculator models the anchor-buyer subsidy model — where the large buyer (Tata, Mahindra, Reliance, etc.) funds digitization of their Tier 2/3 supplier network, then recoups the investment through prevented disruptions. Typical payback: under 6 months. CSR-eligible under Companies Act 2013 Section 135.
Three tiers of Tier 2/3 visibility — each with its own economics
Tier A (baseline): The PathSetu app on the supplier's existing Android phone. Zero hardware. Covers 80%+ of Tier 2/3 use cases — GPS, shock via phone accelerometer, photo proof, voice reports, NFC check-ins, machine-sound anomaly. Anchor buyer may sponsor ~₹200/month data to guarantee uptime.

Tier B & C (optional add-ons): For specific cargo types where the phone alone is insufficient — cold-chain pharma needs precision BLE temperature tags; fragile electronics need continuous BLE shock sensors independent of driver phone orientation; high-value last-mile cargo needs tamper-proof 4G GPS trackers on the vehicle. These add one-time hardware costs, subsidized by the anchor buyer.

CSR compliance — why this is legally real in India
Under Companies Act 2013 Section 135, anchor-buyer subsidy programs for Tier 2/3 digitization qualify as CSR expenditure
✓ Schedule VII clause (ii) — "Employment-enhancing vocation skills... and livelihood enhancement projects." Funding Tier 2/3 digitization directly enhances supplier livelihoods.
✓ Schedule VII clause (ix) — "Contributions to technology incubators" and related innovation activities. Sensor kit deployment fits within this clause when structured through an eligible entity.
✓ 2% mandate applies — Companies with turnover ≥ ₹1,000 crore / net worth ≥ ₹500 crore / net profit ≥ ₹5 crore must spend 2% of 3-year average net profit on CSR. PathSetu subsidy programs fit within existing obligation — not incremental budget ask.
✓ Structuring note: Implementation typically via Section 8 company, registered trust, or registered society (12A + 80G). Ministry of Corporate Affairs has clarified Schedule VII is an illustrative list to be interpreted liberally.
Legal references: Companies Act 2013 Section 135; Schedule VII; Companies (CSR Policy) Amendment Rules, 2021. Tax treatment varies by structuring; consult a qualified advisor. Not legal or tax advice.
